# LME Metals Intelligence Briefing — 2026-06-30

# Executive Brief: Key Structural Shifts and Price Dynamics in Today's LME Non-Ferrous Metals Market

## Critical Structural Shifts Identified:

- **Aluminum:** The recent tightening of supply due to production cuts by major miners like Alcoa has led to a significant shift in the aluminum market structure. This structural change is characterized by a strong backwardation, with near-term contracts trading at a premium compared to the longer-dated futures.

- **Copper:** The copper market has experienced a notable shift towards contango, particularly in the forward curves of 6 to 18 months. This shift indicates a more balanced supply-demand situation and a decrease in short-term physical buying pressure.

- **Zinc:** Zinc market structure has exhibited a strong backwardation due to heightened concerns about production cuts from Glencore and other major miners. This structural change suggests that the prompt spread is widening, indicating a tighter physical market ahead.

## Severe Prompt Spread Moves (Contango/Backwardation):

- **Aluminum:** The 3M/1Y prompt spread has moved into strong backwardation, with the March 2024 contract trading at a significant premium compared to the nearby March 2023 contract. This indicates a tight physical market and potential for arbitrage opportunities.

- **Copper:** The 3M/6M prompt spread has shifted towards contango, suggesting a more balanced supply-demand situation in the near term. However, the 9M/12M spread remains in slight backwardation, indicating potential short-term tightening.

- **Zinc:** The 1Y/2Y prompt spread has shown strong backwardation, with the March 2023 contract trading at a substantial premium compared to the nearby March 2024 contract. This suggests a tight physical market and potential for significant price differentials.

## Absolute Arbitrage or Physical Momentum Flags:

- **Aluminum:** The rapid widening of the backwardation in the aluminum forward curve has created attractive arbitrage opportunities for traders. The near-term contracts are trading at a substantial premium compared to the longer-dated futures, indicating strong physical buying pressure.

- **Copper:** Despite the shift towards contango, there are still some pockets of backwardation in the copper forward curve, particularly in the 6-12 month range. These pockets may offer limited arbitrage opportunities for traders with access to the physical market.

- **Zinc:** The strong backwardation in the zinc prompt spread has led to significant price differentials between nearby and longer-dated contracts. This situation presents a clear arbitrage opportunity for those able to physically deliver metal at the short-end of the curve.

## Recommendations:

1. **Aluminum:** Continue monitoring the tight aluminum market structure and capitalize on potential arbitrage opportunities arising from the strong backwardation in near-term contracts.

2. **Copper:** Keep an eye on any further shifts towards backwardation in the 6-12 month range, as this may indicate a return of physical buying pressure in the short term.

3. **Zinc:** Look for ways to exploit the substantial price differentials created by the zinc market's strong backwardation, particularly in the prompt spread.

In conclusion, today's LME non-ferrous metals market has displayed significant structural shifts and potential arbitrage opportunities. Traders should closely monitor these developments and adjust their strategies accordingly to capitalize on the changing dynamics in each market.
